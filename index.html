More on public charge:


Benefits currently considered for public charge include:
  • SSI Supplemental Security Income provides cash to meet basic needs for aged, blind, and disabled people, who have little or no income.
  • General Assistance A welfare program that benefit adults without dependents.
  • CalWORKS CalWORKs is a public assistance program that provides cash aid and services to eligible families that have a child(ren) in the home.
  • CAPI Cash Assistance Program for Immigrants is a program that provides money to people who can’t get SSI because of their immigration status. You must be an aged, blind, or disabled California resident.
  • Long-term care paid by Medicaid, such as nursing homes
Use of certain benefits is only one factor. Immigration will also consider your health, age, education, skills, employment, current income and sponsor’s income when deciding if you will be a public charge.

Proposed changes to public charge will likely consider:
  • SNAP/food stamps SNAP is a federal nutrition program that can be used to purchase food. SNAP benefits are given to you each month on an EBT plastic card.
  • Non-emergency Medicaid
  • Federal housing/Section 8 A program providing financial assistance for any housing that meets the requirements of the program.
  • Medicare Part D subsidies Medicare Part D is a voluntary prescription drug benefit for people with Medicare, provided through private plans approved by the federal government. It can supplement Medicare.
These changes might never be made. If they are, you will have time to stop these benefits before they are in place.
